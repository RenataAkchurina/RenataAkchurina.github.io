Ulm Town Hall (Rathaus)

Updated March 28, 2017

Town hall of Ulm – is one of the prettiest buildings in the historical center of Ulm, located on the market square near Ulm Minster. The early renaissance building is richly decorated with sculptures and frescoes.

The oldest part of the Town hall was built in 1370, but documentary sources tell us that in 1383 the building was called as a “New trading house”, mentioned for the first time as the Town hall in 1419.

In the 15th century, gothic windows were constructed on the east and the south sides with sculptures of six prince-electors standing at each side of the windows.

In 1520, unique astronomical clock with analemma was placed under the roof of the Town hall. In 1540, the building was reconstructed, where arcades were added on the north facade. On the east facade on the old part of the building, there is the prettiest wall painting by the German painter Martin Schaffner dated by the 16th century, it illustrates virtue and vice, and scenes from the Old Testament.

During the Second World War, after air bombing in December 1944, the Town hall building was severely damaged by heavy fire, however, the ground floor and the south wing of the first floor remained intact.

One of the notable elements of the Town hall of Ulm is the aircraft model created by Albrecht Ludwig Berblinger, who was known as a “Tailor of Ulm”.
