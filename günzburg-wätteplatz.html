Günzburg

Schweinchenbrunnen am Wätteplatz in Günzburg

Updated March 14, 2017

The first water supply system of the town of Günzburg was a water stream in the upper part of the town. Construction of private wells by the house owners and the water supplied from the tube-well on the marketplace led to the creation of impounded area used by the citizens for washing of their livestock. As the water was shallow, this place was called “Wätter” (shallow water in German). In 1792, because of a bad smell, the decision was made to fill in this place, that later, and until the 60s of the 20th century, became used as a Tuesday Pig market.

In 1990, the bronze monument of a pig seller with three young pigs and a spring-well, the monument called “Schweinchenbrunnen am Watterplatz”, appeared on the former market place.

The monument was placed on the crossroad of Wätterplaz and Münzgasse. Some historical researchers suggest that here was a Jewish community with a synagogue located on the place of the current Bankhaus building. Nevertheless, in 1559 Holy Roman Emperor, Ferdinand I expelled the Jewish people from Günzburg. They had to migrate to other places, that became the end for their community in this region.
